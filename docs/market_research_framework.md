# Market Research Framework: AI Recruitment Platform Validation

## Research Objective
Validate the pain point that traditional skill-matching in tech recruiting leads to expensive mis-hires, and prove that learning velocity is a better predictor of success.

## Key Metrics to Validate

### 1. Cost of Bad Hire
**Target Metric**: $240K+ per senior developer mis-hire
**Research Sources**:
- SHRM (Society for Human Resource Management) reports
- Glassdoor hiring cost studies
- Tech recruiting agency data
- Internal company HR metrics

**Validation Method**:
- Survey 50+ tech hiring managers
- Calculate: (Salary + Benefits + Onboarding + Replacement Cost + Lost Productivity)

### 2. Skill Obsolescence Rate
**Target Metric**: 40%+ of "required skills" in job posts become outdated within 18 months
**Research Sources**:
- Stack Overflow Developer Survey (annual)
- GitHub trending technologies data
- Job board analysis (Indeed, LinkedIn, AngelList)
- Framework/library adoption curves

**Validation Method**:
- Analyze 1000+ job postings from 2022 vs 2024
- Track technology mention frequency changes
- Interview developers about skill relevance

### 3. Time-to-Productivity Gap
**Target Metric**: Learning-velocity hires reach productivity 30%+ faster than credential-based hires
**Research Sources**:
- Customer interviews with engineering managers
- Performance review data correlation
- Onboarding timeline analysis
- Peer review feedback systems

**Validation Method**:
- Track 100+ recent hires across multiple companies
- Measure time from hire to first meaningful contribution
- Correlate with hiring criteria used

### 4. Retention Correlation
**Target Metric**: Adaptable learners have 25%+ higher retention rates
**Research Sources**:
- HR analytics platforms
- Employee satisfaction surveys
- Exit interview data
- Career progression tracking

**Validation Method**:
- 2-year retention analysis
- Correlate with initial assessment scores
- Track internal mobility and promotion rates

## Primary Research Plan

### Phase 1: Secondary Research (Week 1)
- **Stack Overflow Developer Survey Analysis**: Extract skill trend data
- **Industry Report Review**: SHRM, Deloitte, McKinsey hiring studies
- **Competitive Analysis**: Existing recruiting tools and their limitations
- **Academic Research**: Studies on learning agility and job performance

### Phase 2: Primary Research (Week 2-3)
- **Customer Interviews**: 20 tech hiring managers (30-min structured interviews)
- **Developer Surveys**: 200+ developers on skill evolution and learning habits
- **Recruiter Insights**: 10 technical recruiters on current pain points
- **HR Analytics**: Partner with 3-5 companies for hiring outcome data

### Phase 3: Data Analysis (Week 4)
- **Statistical Validation**: Prove correlation between learning velocity and performance
- **ROI Calculation**: Quantify potential savings from better hiring decisions
- **Market Sizing**: Calculate TAM/SAM for learning-velocity recruiting tools
- **Competitive Positioning**: Define unique value proposition vs existing solutions

## Interview Scripts

### Hiring Manager Interview Questions
1. "What's the biggest challenge in hiring senior developers?"
2. "How often do 'experienced' hires struggle with new technologies?"
3. "What's the cost when a senior hire doesn't work out?"
4. "How do you currently assess learning ability vs static skills?"
5. "Would you pay more for candidates proven to adapt quickly?"

### Developer Interview Questions
1. "How often do your day-to-day skills change?"
2. "What's more valuable: 5 years React experience or recent hackathon wins?"
3. "How do you stay current with technology changes?"
4. "What predicts success in new roles better: credentials or learning speed?"

## Success Criteria
- **Statistical Significance**: 95% confidence in key metrics
- **Market Validation**: 70%+ of interviewees confirm the pain point
- **Competitive Differentiation**: Clear advantage over 3+ existing solutions
- **Business Case**: ROI calculation showing 3x+ return on platform investment

## Timeline
- **Week 1**: Secondary research and data collection
- **Week 2**: Customer interviews and surveys
- **Week 3**: Data analysis and validation
- **Week 4**: Final report and business case development

This framework will provide the data foundation needed to validate our "Learning Velocity over Static Skills" value proposition and support our go-to-market strategy.
